# Engineering Career Ladder & Team Building

## Individual Contributor Track

### Level 1: Junior Engineer (0-2 years)
**Scope**: Tasks and bug fixes under guidance
**Technical Skills**:
- Writes clean, tested code with review feedback
- Learns codebase and development workflow
- Debugs issues with guidance
- Follows established patterns and practices

**Behaviors**:
- Asks questions, seeks help appropriately
- Meets commitments, communicates blockers early
- Receptive to feedback, shows growth

**Promotion Criteria**: Consistently delivers assigned work, needs less guidance over time

---

### Level 2: Software Engineer (2-4 years)
**Scope**: Features within a well-defined area
**Technical Skills**:
- Designs and implements features independently
- Writes maintainable, well-tested code
- Participates meaningfully in code reviews
- Understands system architecture at component level

**Behaviors**:
- Takes ownership of deliverables
- Communicates progress and risks proactively
- Helps onboard new team members
- Identifies and proposes improvements

**Promotion Criteria**: Delivers features reliably, starts influencing technical decisions

---

### Level 3: Senior Engineer (4-7 years)
**Scope**: Complex features, cross-component work
**Technical Skills**:
- Designs systems considering scalability, security, maintainability
- Makes sound technical trade-offs
- Identifies and addresses technical debt
- Debugs complex production issues
- Mentors junior engineers effectively

**Behaviors**:
- Leads projects from design to delivery
- Influences team technical direction
- Builds relationships across teams
- Documents decisions and knowledge shares

**Promotion Criteria**: Recognized technical leader on the team, multiplies team output

---

### Level 4: Staff Engineer (7-10+ years)
**Scope**: Multi-team systems, platform-wide impact
**Technical Skills**:
- Designs and evolves large-scale systems
- Sets technical direction for product area
- Solves ambiguous, high-impact problems
- Evaluates build vs buy decisions
- Deep expertise in multiple domains

**Behaviors**:
- Influences engineering-wide practices
- Mentors senior engineers
- Partners with product and leadership on strategy
- Represents company externally (conferences, hiring)

**Promotion Criteria**: Organization-wide technical impact, trusted advisor to leadership

---

### Level 5: Principal/Distinguished Engineer (10-15+ years)
**Scope**: Company-wide, industry influence
**Technical Skills**:
- Defines multi-year technical strategy
- Solves novel, company-critical problems
- Industry recognition in specialty area
- Anticipates technology trends

**Behaviors**:
- Shapes engineering culture
- Executive-level communication
- Mentors staff engineers into leadership
- External thought leadership

**Promotion Criteria**: Exceptional individual who shapes company's technical future

---

## Management Track

### Engineering Manager
**Scope**: Single team (5-10 engineers)
**Responsibilities**:
- Hiring, onboarding, performance management
- Career development for team members
- Project delivery and team productivity
- Technical guidance (still technical, ~20-30% hands-on)
- Cross-functional collaboration

**Key Metrics**:
- Team velocity and delivery predictability
- Team health (retention, engagement)
- Quality metrics (bugs, incidents)
- Growth: promotions, skill development

---

### Senior Engineering Manager
**Scope**: Multiple teams or large team (10-20 engineers)
**Responsibilities**:
- Manages managers and/or senior ICs
- Owns technical roadmap for area
- Resource allocation and prioritization
- Process improvement at department level
- Stakeholder management

**Key Metrics**:
- Cross-team delivery coordination
- Manager development
- Technical debt reduction
- Operational excellence

---

### Director of Engineering
**Scope**: Department (20-50 engineers)
**Responsibilities**:
- Organizational design and team structure
- Hiring strategy and employer brand
- Budget management
- Executive stakeholder relationships
- Technical strategy for product area

**Key Metrics**:
- Department-level OKRs
- Retention and recruiting funnel
- Budget adherence
- Strategic initiative delivery

---

### VP of Engineering
**Scope**: Entire engineering organization (50-200+ engineers)
**Responsibilities**:
- Engineering vision and culture
- Executive team partnership
- Company-wide technical decisions
- Investor and board communication
- M&A technical diligence

**Key Metrics**:
- Company-level engineering metrics
- Executive alignment
- Organizational health
- Industry positioning

---

## Hiring Playbook

### Sourcing Strategy
1. **Referrals** (40%): Best quality, incentivize generously
2. **Inbound** (25%): Employer brand, job boards, LinkedIn
3. **Outbound** (25%): Recruiting team sourcing
4. **Events** (10%): Conferences, meetups, university relations

### Interview Process

**Stage 1: Recruiter Screen (30 min)**
- Role fit and motivation
- Compensation alignment
- Timeline and availability

**Stage 2: Hiring Manager Screen (45 min)**
- Technical background deep dive
- Career goals and growth areas
- Culture and values alignment
- Answer candidate questions

**Stage 3: Technical Interviews (2-3 hours)**
- Coding/problem solving (1 hour)
- System design (1 hour, senior+ only)
- Domain deep dive (45 min, relevant experience)

**Stage 4: Team Fit (1-2 hours)**
- Cross-functional partner (PM, Design)
- Peer engineer
- Values and collaboration assessment

**Stage 5: Executive/Final (30-45 min)**
- Senior+ roles: VP or CTO
- Sell the opportunity
- Address concerns

### Interview Rubric

| Dimension | Weight | Signals |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Technical Skill | 30% | Problem solving, code quality, system thinking |
| Experience Fit | 25% | Relevant domain, scale, technology |
| Communication | 20% | Clarity, listening, collaboration |
| Growth Potential | 15% | Learning agility, ambition, self-awareness |
| Culture Fit | 10% | Values alignment, team dynamics |

### Offer Strategy
- Competitive base (50th-75th percentile)
- Meaningful equity for senior+ roles
- Sign-on bonus for competitive situations
- Sell the mission, team, and growth opportunity

## Performance Management

### Feedback Cadence
- **Weekly 1:1s**: Tactical, blockers, quick feedback
- **Monthly**: Career conversation, growth areas
- **Quarterly**: Goal review, formal feedback
- **Annual**: Performance review, compensation adjustment

### Performance Dimensions
1. **Results**: Delivery against goals
2. **Technical Growth**: Skill development
3. **Collaboration**: Cross-functional effectiveness
4. **Leadership**: Influence and mentorship
5. **Culture**: Values embodiment

### Addressing Underperformance
1. Direct conversation with specific examples
2. Written improvement plan (PIP) with clear expectations
3. Weekly check-ins during improvement period (30-60 days)
4. Decision point: improve, role change, or exit
5. Document throughout for clarity and fairness

## Retention Strategies

### Why Engineers Leave
1. Lack of growth and challenge
2. Poor management
3. Below-market compensation
4. Misalignment with company direction
5. Work-life balance issues
6. Better opportunity elsewhere

### Retention Levers
- **Growth**: Stretch assignments, new technologies, leadership opportunities
- **Compensation**: Regular market adjustments, equity refreshers
- **Recognition**: Public praise, awards, promotions
- **Autonomy**: Decision authority, flexible work
- **Purpose**: Connect work to mission and impact
- **Community**: Team bonds, mentorship, ERGs

### Stay Interview Questions
- What keeps you here?
- What would make you consider leaving?
- What would you change about your role?
- Do you feel you're growing?
- Is there anything I can do better as your manager?
